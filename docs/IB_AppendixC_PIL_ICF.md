# PART 1 — PATIENT INFORMATION LEAFLET

*Please read this leaflet carefully before deciding whether to take part. Take your time. You can ask your doctor or the study team any questions before you decide.*

---

## What is this study about?

We are doing a study to test a new computer tool called **CogAssess**.

CogAssess is a software program that listens to a short speech recording and produces measurements related to memory, speech, and thinking. We want to find out whether these measurements are useful for doctors when they are assessing patients for memory problems.

---

## Why have I been invited?

You have been invited because you are attending this clinic for a memory assessment. We are inviting people aged 50 and over who are having their memory checked.

Taking part is completely voluntary. If you decide not to take part, this will not affect your care in any way.

---

## Do I have to take part?

No. It is entirely your choice. If you decide to take part, you can change your mind and withdraw at any time, without giving a reason, and without any effect on your medical care.

---

## What will happen if I take part?

If you agree to take part, here is what will happen:

**Step 1 — Your usual memory assessment**
Your doctor or clinician will carry out their normal memory tests. These are the same tests you would have had whether or not you are in this study.

**Step 2 — The CogAssess recording (about 15–20 minutes)**
You will be asked to speak into a microphone connected to a computer. The computer will show you simple tasks on the screen — for example, describing your morning routine, or talking about a past event. Each task takes about one to two minutes.

You do not need any special skills or preparation. There are no right or wrong answers. We just want to hear you speak naturally.

**Step 3 — Your doctor reviews the results**
After you finish speaking, the CogAssess software will automatically produce a report. Your doctor will look at the report alongside the results of your usual memory tests.

**Step 4 — Optional follow-up visit (3 months later)**
We may ask you to come back in three months to repeat the recording. This is optional — you can still take part in the study even if you do not want to return for the follow-up.

---

## Will CogAssess tell me if I have dementia?

**No.** CogAssess does not diagnose dementia or any other condition. It produces measurements that may be useful information for your doctor — but your doctor makes all clinical decisions. The results from CogAssess will not change your diagnosis on their own.

Your doctor will discuss any relevant findings with you in the usual way.

---

## What are the possible benefits of taking part?

There may be no direct benefit to you personally. However, the information collected in this study may help to improve how doctors assess memory problems in the future, which may benefit other patients.

---

## Are there any risks or disadvantages?

The assessment is non-invasive — we only ask you to speak. There are no injections, no physical procedures, and no known physical risks.

Some people find memory assessments tiring or slightly anxiety-provoking. If you feel uncomfortable at any time, you can stop and rest, or withdraw from the session entirely.

The CogAssess software uses artificial intelligence to analyse speech. Like all AI systems, it may not perform equally well for all people. Your doctor is aware of this and will always make decisions based on the full picture, not on CogAssess results alone.

---

## What happens to my information?

Your privacy is very important to us. Here is how we protect your information:

**Pseudonymisation:** You will be given a code number for this study. Only your doctor and the study team at this site can link your code number to your identity. CogAssess stores your code number only — your name is never entered into the system.

**Your voice recordings:** Your speech is processed automatically by the CogAssess software immediately after recording. Once processing is complete, the audio file is deleted. Your voice is not stored anywhere.

**Your data:** The measurements (scores) produced by CogAssess, along with your memory test scores and some basic information about you (age band, gender, language), will be stored securely and shared with the study Sponsor (MemoryTell Ltd) in anonymised form.

**Your rights under GDPR:** Under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), you have the right to:
- Access the personal data we hold about you
- Ask us to correct any mistakes
- Ask us to delete your data (subject to legal obligations to retain research data)
- Withdraw your consent at any time

The legal basis for processing your data in this study is **Article 9(2)(j) GDPR** (processing for scientific research purposes in the public interest), subject to appropriate safeguards.

For questions about your data, contact the Data Controller at your site or the Sponsor's Data Protection Officer at: **dpo@memorytell.com**

**How long will my data be kept?** Anonymised research data will be retained for 15 years in line with Good Clinical Practice requirements. No identifiable data will be retained after the study ends.

---

## Will my GP or family doctor be informed?

With your permission, we will inform your GP that you took part in this study. This is standard practice in clinical research. If you would prefer that your GP is not informed, please let the study team know.

---

## What if something goes wrong?

If you have a concern about any aspect of this study, please contact the study team in the first instance (contact details below). If you feel your concern has not been addressed, you can contact the Ethics Committee that approved this study.

In the unlikely event that you are harmed as a result of taking part in this study, the Sponsor has appropriate insurance in place. Nothing in this leaflet limits your legal rights.

---

## Who is funding this study?

This study is funded by MemoryTell Ltd, a medical technology company. St John Lynch & Co. Ltd developed the CogAssess software on behalf of MemoryTell Ltd.

---

## Who has reviewed this study?

This study has been reviewed and approved by an Independent Ethics Committee. Details of the approving committee are available from the study team on request.
